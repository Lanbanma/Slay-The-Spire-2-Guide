Ironclad
Build Guide

The Ironclad is Slay the Spire 2's powerhouse warrior — block-stacking, Strength-scaling, and burning through decks with Exhaust synergies. This guide covers the two strongest archetypes, every card and relic worth knowing, and how to counter the bosses that punish you for playing them.

Ironclad

HP: 80 — Ascension: 75

Character Overview

Strengths

  • • Highest single-target damage potential in the game
  • • Strength scaling enables infinite-like turns late
  • • Exhaust synergies snowball quickly once online
  • • Multiple viable archetypes — highly adaptable to draft

Weaknesses

  • • Vulnerable to Strength-reducing bosses (Donu & Deca)
  • • Exhaust builds can flood hand with dead cards early
  • • Limited AoE options without specific relic support
  • • Block generation is inconsistent before Act 2

Core Mechanic

The Ironclad's unique mechanic is Burn Healing — when a Burn card is exhausted, the Ironclad heals 6 HP. While less central than in StS1, it still underpins survivability and synergizes with Exhaust builds in later acts.

Build #1

Strength Build

Scale damage once, attack forever

The Strength Build revolves around stacking the Strength buff as quickly as possible, then hitting with heavy attacks that scale proportionally. Even a modest +8 Strength turns Bash into a 15-damage hit. At +20 and above with Limit Break, the Ironclad can one-shot most bosses in a single turn.

♠ Core Cards

Inflame

Attack Power

Gain 2 Strength. Upgraded: Gain 3 Strength. Your highest-priority pick — an early Inflame can carry the entire run.

Limit Break

Skill Exhaust

Double your Strength. Upgraded: no longer Exhausts. Once upgraded, loop it every turn to reach absurd damage ceilings.

Heavy Blade

Attack

Deal 14 damage. Strength applies 3× (5× upgraded). Your primary finisher — at +15 Strength, this hits for 59 / 89 damage.

Demon Form

Power

Gain 2 Strength at the start of each turn (3 upgraded). Passive Strength that renders long boss fights trivially easy.

Pommel Strike

Attack

9 damage, draw 1 card (2 upgraded). Acts as draw and damage simultaneously — keeps hands flowing mid-combat.

Armaments

Skill

Gain 5 Block, upgrade 1 card in hand. Upgraded: upgrade all cards in hand. Essential for upgrading Heavy Blade mid-combat.

◈ Essential Relics

⚔

Vajra

Start each combat with 1 Strength. Modest alone; combined with Inflame and Demon Form it means your first Heavy Blade is noticeably stronger.

🪬

Akabeko

Your first attack each combat deals 8 extra damage. Pairs perfectly with Heavy Blade as your opener — near-essential in Act 1 runs.

🔮

Mark of Pain

Gain +1 Energy and draw a Wound every turn. Feeds Exhaust synergy if you also run Feel No Pain and Dark Embrace.

💎

Philosopher's Stone

All enemies gain 1 Strength. In return you start with +1 Energy permanently. Risky but the energy gain makes Limit Break → Heavy Blade turns viable on 3 energy.

⬡ Recommended Potions

💪

Strength Potion

+3 Strength this combat. Doubles Heavy Blade output mid-fight.

⚡

Energy Potion

+2 Energy this turn. Fuels Limit Break → Heavy Blade combos on tight turns.

🔥

Essence of Steel

Gain 4 Plated Armour. A lifesaver when burst damage is needed but Strength isn't online yet.

Build #2

Exhaust Build

Every card played is fuel

The Exhaust Build turns every card leaving your hand into a resource. Feel No Pain gives Block on each Exhaust. Dark Embrace refills your hand. Evolve turns curses and wounds into opportunities. When this build clicks, you're gaining 10+ Block and drawing 2 cards every time you play a Burn or Offering.

♠ Core Cards

Feel No Pain

Power

Gain 3 Block whenever you Exhaust a card (4 upgraded). The backbone — stack 2 copies and each Offering gives you 6–8 Block total.

Dark Embrace

Power

Draw 1 card whenever you Exhaust a card. The engine. Every Exhaust is a net draw — keeping you in cards while thinning your deck simultaneously.

Offering

Skill Exhaust

Lose 6 HP, gain 2 Energy, draw 3 cards. Triggers both Feel No Pain and Dark Embrace — a single Offering nets you energy, cards, AND block.

Fiend Fire

Attack Exhaust

Exhaust your hand, deal 7 damage per card Exhausted. Your finisher — with 5 cards in hand this hits for 35 damage while triggering Feel No Pain 5 times.

Evolve

Power

Draw 1 card when you draw a Status card (2 upgraded). Turns Wounds and Burns from liabilities into extra draw every cycle.

Second Wind

Skill

Exhaust all non-Attack cards in hand, gain 5 Block per card Exhausted. Emergency survival button that also triggers Feel No Pain per card.

◈ Essential Relics

🔱

Dead Branch

Whenever you Exhaust a card, add a random card to your hand. An Exhaust build's best relic — each Fiend Fire generates entire turns worth of new cards.

⚗️

Gamblers Chip

At the start of combat, if you have 3 or fewer cards in hand, draw until you have 5. Prevents slow Exhaust starts where you're waiting for key powers.

🎭

Runic Cube

Whenever you lose HP, draw 1 card. Offering costs you 6 HP — which now also draws a card. Significantly amplifies Offering's already-insane value.

🗡️

Calipers

Don't lose more than 15 Block at end of turn. Preserves the huge Block stacks you generate through Feel No Pain, making tanking easier across multiple rounds.

⬡ Recommended Potions

💊

Block Potion

Instant 12 Block. Buy time while waiting for Feel No Pain to appear.

🌀

Distilled Chaos

Play top 3 cards of draw pile. Can chain into Offerings and Exhaust triggers unexpectedly.

❤️

Fairy in a Bottle

Revive at 30% HP if you die. Insurance for the risky Offering + low HP decisions.

Play Strategy

1

Act 1 — Build the Engine

  • ▸Prioritise Inflame above almost everything. A first-floor Inflame changes the entire run.
  • ▸Take card removal aggressively — a 10-card deck with Inflame beats a 20-card deck without it.
  • ▸Skip most Wounds in shops unless they're less than 80 gold.
  • ▸Beat the Gremlin Nob by front-loading Block before attacking.
2

Act 2 — Pivot & Commit

  • ▸By now you should know if you're Strength or Exhaust. Don't try to do both.
  • ▸Heavy Blade and Fiend Fire should both be upgraded if they're in your deck.
  • ▸Limit Break (unupgraded) is still worth playing as a one-time doubler in boss fights.
  • ▸Don't neglect Block options — Act 2 elites punish passive players.
3

Act 3 — Execute the Win

  • ▸Your deck should be 15 cards or fewer. If it's larger, visit every removal event.
  • ▸Limit Break upgraded turns any boss fight into a math problem the boss loses.
  • ▸Save Strength and Energy potions for Act 3 boss — the Heart needs a 1-turn kill plan.
  • ▸Demon Form + upgraded Limit Break = win condition. Anything else is supplemental.
♦

Draft Priorities

  • ▸S-tier picks: Inflame, Limit Break, Dark Embrace, Feel No Pain
  • ▸A-tier picks: Demon Form, Fiend Fire, Heavy Blade, Offering
  • ▸B-tier: Pummel, Pommel Strike, Second Wind, Evolve
  • ▸Skip low-value Block cards unless you have nothing — Impervious is the exception.

General Rule

Upgrade Heavy Blade or Limit Break before anything else. A +1 on Bash is nice. Upgrading Heavy Blade from 3× Strength to 5× Strength at +15 Strength is an extra 30 damage per swing.

Key Combos

The Ironclad's best turns are chains, not single cards. Learn these sequences and you'll know exactly what to hold for and when to snap off your finisher.

The Classic Burst

Demon Form → Limit Break ×2 → Heavy Blade ×3

With Demon Form active (+2 Strength/turn) and upgraded Limit Break: on turn 3 you might have +16 Strength, double it to +32, Heavy Blade hits for 14 + (32 × 5) = 174 damage. Most bosses have under 300 HP.

The Exhaust Engine Turn

Feel No Pain ×2 + Dark Embrace → Offering → Fiend Fire

Offering: −6 HP, +2 Energy, draw 3. Each Feel No Pain gives 3 Block (6 total). Dark Embrace draws another card. Then Fiend Fire exhausts the whole hand — each card triggers Feel No Pain again for massive block + 7 damage per card.

Dead Branch Chaos

Dead Branch relic + Feel No Pain → Fiend Fire

Each Exhaust from Fiend Fire generates a random card via Dead Branch. Those cards can themselves be exhausted in the same chain if they are naturally Exhaust cards. With 6+ cards in hand, you can generate an entirely new hand mid-Fiend Fire.

The Safe Opener

Shrug It Off → Defend → Inflame → Bash

Against high-damage openers (Jaw Worm, Lagavulin), spend turn 1 front-loading block first. Then play Inflame before Bash to benefit from the +2 Strength on the attack itself — never waste a buff by playing attacks first.

Boss & Elite Counter Tips

The Ironclad's damage ceiling is the highest in the game — but several bosses specifically counter Strength scaling. Know the matchups before you get there.

Act Bosses

💀

The Guardian

Act 1 Boss

Activates a second phase at 50% HP with higher damage. The key is front-loading Block before its Defensive Mode, then unloading Heavy Blades during the vulnerability window.

✓ Kill before phase 2 if possible ⚡ Save Strength Potion for vulnerability ✗ Don't attack during Defensive Mode
💀

Donu & Deca

Act 2 Boss

Deca reduces your Strength by 2 per turn. This is the Strength Build's hardest matchup. Kill Deca first, then mop up Donu. The Exhaust Build handles this better — its damage doesn't rely on Strength.

✓ Kill Deca first, always ⚡ Exhaust build significantly easier here ✗ Don't spread damage between both
💀

Awakened One

Act 3 Boss

Heals to full and gains 2 Strength per power you play after reviving. Play all your powers before killing it in phase 1, or hold them for phase 2 if you can't. Alternatively, kill it before it reaches half HP — save energy and burst it.

✓ Play all powers before first kill ⚡ Save burst for phase 2 if uncertain ✗ Never play Demon Form after phase 2 starts

Key Elite Encounters

⚡

Gremlin Nob

Gains 3 Strength whenever you play a Skill. Do not play a single Skill — only Attacks and Powers. An Inflame before Bashes means he dies to 2 good attacks with no counter-scaling.

😴

Lagavulin

Debuffs your Strength and Dexterity by −2 each after 3 turns asleep. Wake it by attacking on turn 1. Never let the debuff tick — deal 8+ damage early to trigger the combat immediately.

👻

Sentries

Three enemies that Daze and Wound your deck. Whirlwind or Cleave is ideal here. Without AoE, prioritise killing one immediately and blocking the others — don't let Dazed cards multiply for more than one cycle.

🔵

Shield & Spear

Shield removes your Buffs when attacked, Spear deals high damage. Kill Spear first — then Shield can't punish you by removing Strength while you chip it down safely.

You're ready to climb.

The Ironclad rewards commitment to a single archetype. Pick your build on floor 2 and draft everything toward it. A focused 15-card deck beats a versatile 30-card deck every time.
