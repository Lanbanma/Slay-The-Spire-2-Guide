C

Avoid These

Common traps for new players

Strike & Defend (all characters)

Your starting cards. Remove at every opportunity — a 10-card deck with 0 Strikes beats a 20-card deck every time by cycling faster to your win condition.

Flex (Ironclad)

The temporary Strength misleads new players. It only helps attacks played in the same turn. Inflame does the same job permanently for 1 Energy.

Zap (Defect)

Costs 1 Energy to channel 1 Lightning with no damage. Ball Lightning does exactly the same thing but also deals 7 damage. Never choose Zap.

Consecrate (Watcher)

5 AoE with no stance effect wastes a card slot that should go to stance-swap cards. The Watcher's damage model doesn't need cheap filler attacks.

Sword Boomerang

Random targeting wastes damage on near-dead enemies. The Ironclad needs focused burst, not scatter damage — skip this entirely.

For New Players

Quick Draft Guide

You don't need to memorise every tier on your first run. These five rules cover 90% of draft decisions and stop the most common early mistakes.

Smaller deck = faster wins

Every card you add risks drawing it at the wrong moment. Aim for 10–15 cards by Act 2. Skip anything that isn't better than half your deck.

Remove Strikes and Defends first

Use every removal event and pay shop prices to cut your starting cards. A lean deck finds win conditions consistently.

Upgrade your win-condition card first

A +1 on Bash is nice. Upgrading Limit Break to remove Exhaust, or Catalyst to triple Poison — those are game-changing. Do those first at Campfire.

Commit to one archetype

If you're going Strength, skip Exhaust cards and vice versa. Mixed builds are unreliable. Decide on floor 3 and draft everything toward it.
